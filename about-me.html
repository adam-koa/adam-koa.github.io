About Me

At my core, I'm an engineer. I like understanding how things work, and using that knowledge to make them better.


I graduated from the University of Toronto in 2021 with a degree in Mechanical Engineering. My coursework specialized in Mechatronics and Energy & the Environment, and I completed an additional Minor in Sustainable Energy and Certificate in Engineering Business. I was involved with several design teams and clubs, most prominently the Sustainable Engineers Association.


As a mechanical engineer, I'm familiar with a handful of computer-aided design (CAD) softwares and simulation tools (primarily in the fluid dynamics realm). I'm also experienced with rapid manufacturing and small-scale prototyping, including both in terms of mechanical design and basic electronics.


In the workplace, I have often had to push myself beyond just being a mechanical engineer, and as a result have become quite adept with a wide range of programming languages and software topics. I am frequently my team's "go-to" data analytics person, likely due to my naturally analytical way of approaching problem-solving.


While a student at the University of Toronto, I received several awards for academic achievement; I was also part of a capstone design team in my final year that won the John W. Senders Award for Imaginative Design, which is awarded to each year's top medical design project.


I received a Certificate of Basic Machining after completing a two-day course at George Brown College, in which I learned the basics of operating a lathe and mill. I also studied for and passed a 3D-modeling course that tested competency using SolidWorks.


Outside of work and school, I liked to spend much of my free time playing music, keeping myself healthy, and experimenting in the kitchen. After a recent trip to Switzerland, I've also become more interested in languages, and am slowly working my way through Duolingo's German course.
