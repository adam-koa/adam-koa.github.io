About Me

At my core, I'm a problem-solver. I like figuring out how things work, and using that knowledge to make them better.


I graduated from the University of Toronto (UofT) in 2021 with a degree in Mechanical Engineering. UofT's coursework was broad, and exposed me to a wide range of topics within mechanical engineering (e.g., fluid mechanics, thermodynamics, manufacturing, solid mechanics, etc.). During my time at school, I became particularly interested in renewable energy and energy systems; as a result, many of my elective courses were focused on the energy, such a Alternative Energy Systems, Energy Policy, and Introduction to Fusion Energy. Furthermore, my growing interest in sustainability led me to join the Sustainable Engineers Association (SEA) in my second year, which I remained an executive at for the duration of my time at UofT. While part of SEA, I helped organize sustainability-focused events, including an annual career fair.


My interests in energy and sustainability carried over from my university days to my career, and they have helped guide the workplaces I've chosen since then. While still a student, I did a 1-year internship at the Independent Electricity System Operator (IESO), a Crown Corporation that oversees Ontario's power system. After graduating, I worked on the innovation team at Alectra Utilities, where I helped to design pilot projects to test the feasibility of implementing clean energy technologies. And most recently, I've been a Mechatronics Engineer at ReDeTec, a startup that is seeking to make plastic recycling cheaper, more sustainable, and accessible on a small-scale basis.


Outside of work and school, I liked to spend much of my free time playing music, keeping myself healthy, and experimenting in the kitchen. After a recent trip to Switzerland, I've also become more interested in languages, and am slowly working my way through Duolingo's German course.
