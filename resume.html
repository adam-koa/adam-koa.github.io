May 2022 - June 2024

Toronto, ON

I worked on ReDeTec's research & development team as a jack-of-all-trades mechanical engineer. I designed mechanical and electronics systems, regularly using software tools to create 3d-models or run fluid dynamics simulations. Once designed, I performed additonal analyses to gauge feasibility, including with respect to possible failure modes, manufacturability, and cost. I also procured selected designs from off-the-shelf suppliers or through custom manufacturing (e.g., 3d-printing, CNC machining), built testing rigs for prototypes, and frequently participated in design validation testing.


In addition to my mechanical engineering responsibilities, I was also ReDeTec's de-facto software engineer and data analyst. Using Java, I developed a major revision of the ProtoCycler's control software, which improved performance and added new features. I also created a back-end system that linked the client-side Java program with an AWS database and Shopify system, in order to automatically control access to subscription-based software. I regularly incorporated Python scripts into the R&D team's workflow, creating tools to streamline the process of analyzing testing data. Finally, I coded micro-controller firmware in C++ to control a cooling fan based on thermocouple temperature readings.


Because of ReDeTec's startup nature, miscellaneous tasks frequently arose for which I claimed responsibility: the most prominent of these was the management of the company's digital advertising. Starting with no knowledge of marketing and learning on the fly through online courses and tutorials, I developed advertising campaigns in Google and LinkedIn.

Power Systems Planning Intern

Independent Electricity System Operator (IESO)

May 2019 - May 2020

Toronto, ON

I spent 12 months interning at the Independent Electricity System Operator (IESO), a Crown Corporation that oversees Ontario's electricity market and power grid. My team was responsible for planning the future of Ontario's electricity system, ensuring a safe and reliable flow of electricity in the coming decades.


I supported the development of long-term plans for the electrical grid through the reseach, analysis, and consultation stages. I worked with utility stakeholders across the province to compile historical electrical and climate data, which was then input to statistical models in Python that could generate predictions of future load growth. Using these load forecasts, I conducted power flow simulations to assess the capabilities of the grid under standardized failure conditions, in its current state as well as with various potential transmission or generation solutions. Finally, I assisted with the writing of public-facing reports for Toronto and the Regional Municipality of York.


Beyond system planning, I also helped my team by updating software tools in VBA and Excel, which were used for analyzing power flow data. Furthermore, I created a wide range of maps, single-line electrical diagrams, and one-pagers.
