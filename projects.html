Thesis

Design and Optimization of a Pico-Scale Hydroelectric Turbine

During my final year of undergrad, I wrote a thesis as part of the Water and Energy Research Lab (WERL). The focus of the thesis was on designing and optimizing a pico-scale hydroelectric turbine, which could be used to improve industrial piping efficiency or provide small-scale energy extraction.

Building off of work performed in previous theses, I spent the first half of my year reviewing fluid dynamics theory, learning computational fluid dynamics software, and building an initial 3D model. In the second half of the year, I performed a two-stage Taguchi Design of Experiments (DOE) in order to optimize the geometry of the turbine.

Ball Launcher

4th Year Product Design Course Project

As part of a fourth year course on Product Design, I worked on a team of six engineering students to design and prototype a ping-pong ball launcher. As per the project’s guidelines, the launcher had to conform to a number of specifications and requirements, including size, launch distance, safety, and cost.

During the preliminary design phase, I was responsible for generating an initial concept and performing an economic analysis. I also assisted other team members in creating a Product Functional Decomposition (PFD) diagram and performing Design Failure Mode Effect Analysis (DFMEA). Once a final design concept was chosen, my primary task was to create a 3D-model for the launching tube and mechanism, along with relevant engineering drawings. I was also heavily involved in creating the Design of Experiments (DOE) used to test the launcher’s capabilities. Oscilloscope

3rd Year Mechatronics Course Project

In my third year of undergraduate education, I worked on a design project for my Analog and Digital Electronics for Mechatronics (MIE346) course. The project involved designing and prototyping an oscilloscope could convert both a 15 V and 1.5 V AC input signal into a 0 V – 5 V output signal.

The project began with a conceptual design phase, which resulted in a theoretical design for the analog circuitry involved in the oscilloscope. From this, a PCB layout was created in EAGLE, which represented a physical version of the theoretical circuit. After a prototype was manufactured, this PCB was tested in order to determine any issues that might require updating for the final design. The initial circuit design was then integrated into a broader circuit, which now included digital components. A case for the PCB was also 3D-modeled and 3D-printed. The final PCB was manufactured to create a final prototype, which was integrated into the 3D-printed case.
